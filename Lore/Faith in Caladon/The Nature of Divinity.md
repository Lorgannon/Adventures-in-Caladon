Barring two exceptions, the divine elements of Caladon are largely inscrutable. Very few in living memory can claim to have seen their gods in person, their will normally carried out by their mortal followers. This lack of *physical* presence is very rarely a cause of doubt for any believers however, as the miracles carried out by members of their clergy are difficult to deny. Devout worshippers of  gods may heal the sick, smite the unholy, or shield the innocent depending on their God's domain

The aforementioned exceptions are the twin diarchs of the Tsaibaki empire, who stand as distinctly physical manifestations of divine power. Whether the god-rulers are properly divine is the subject of much debate outside of the empire, but their sheer power is difficult to explain by more mundane means.
