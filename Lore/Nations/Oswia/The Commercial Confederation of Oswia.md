(Brutalist/Steampunk landscape, Smog choked skies):

Oswia was founded as a series of non-competition pacts between various mercantile and manufacturing dynasties and functions essentially as a loose collection of company towns bound together by miles of red tape, bureaucracy, and corporate ties. The average citizen functions as little more than a tool from which their parent company derives profit, and as such, life in Oswia rivals even the Tsaibaki Empire in terms of struggle. Oswia’s intense drive for profit has not been without its benefits however, and Oswian industry far surpasses both Tsaibaki and Altria in its capacity for mass production. Its factories run so efficiently that both of its neighbor nations have long-standing trade and manufacturing agreements with a variety of companies held under the pact. This emphasis on raw industry has had a number of curious side effects. One being that, due to the poor environmental conditions generated by such intense levels of national production, the majority of the rich ruling class of Oswia do not actually live within its borders. Instead choosing to summer permanently in the nations with which their parent company has developed the greatest ties to, and paying representatives and middle managers to shoulder the everyday task of maintaining their means of production.
