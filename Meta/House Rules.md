Overall Game Changes:


* “Story Points”
   * At the beginning of the campaign, each player is given a “Story Point”. This point may, at any time, be used to cause any one roll to automatically succeed or fail. These points recharge at the DM’s discretion, often based on some in-game or real-world time interval. It is best to think of these points as a limited pool of “Deus Ex Machina” moments to guarantee a planned course of action works, or that a heroic sacrifice delivers its intended effect.

Combat Rules:


* Riposte:
   * If a melee attack against a creature with at least one level in a martial class or subclass (Fighter, Barbarian, Monk, Ranger, Paladin, Rogue, Sword/Valor Bard, Bladesinger Wizard, Hexblade Warlock) misses by more than 5, the target may use their reaction to immediately make an attack with their equipped melee weapon (or unarmed if they are holding a ranged weapon). If the attacker rolls a natural one, the riposting attack is rolled with advantage. Casters (or Martials with a casting ability) may use melee spell attacks under the same conditions, provided they also have the “War Caster” feat


* Flanking:
   * Rather than giving simple advantage against a flanked target, flanking now gives a +3 to the attack roll against the target, and upon a hit, allows the attacker to add an extra Damage dice
* Shove:
   * Creatures may choose to shove other creatures using a bonus action rather than an action. When using shove in this way, they may only move the target, not knock it prone.

Class changes:
* Alternative Casting Ability:
   * Warlocks may choose to use intelligence rather than charisma.
   * Sorcerers may choose to use constitution rather than charisma.
   * Paladins may choose to use wisdom rather than charisma.
* Mana Burn (Sorcerers):
   * Starting at first level, Sorcerers may cast known spells at higher levels than their highest available slot would normally allow. Casting in this manner inflicts one level of exhaustion per spell level above the expended slot. Sorcerers may use this feature to cast spells above 9th level. In this case, damage or extra effects are applied as explained in the spell’s description.
* Spell Thief (Arcane Trickster):
   * Arcane Tricksters now gain the Spell Thief feature at level 3
   * Their level 17 feature now allows them to use Spell Thief a number of times equal to their proficiency bonus
* Improved Frenzy (Berserker Barbarian)
   * Entering Frenzy now consumes a second charge of rage instead of adding a stack of exhaustion
   * Mindless Rage now provides resistance to Psychic Damage


* Expanded Spell List (Sword and Valor Bard)
   * Both martial bard classes may now learn Booming Blade and Green Flame-blade.


* Alternative Artificer Spells
   * Artillerists may swap either Thunderwave or Shield for Magic Missile
   * Armorer Artificers may exchange Thunderwave or Magic Missile for Shield 
* Fighter:
   * The Battlemaster subclass has been removed. In its place, all level 3 fighters, regardless of subclass, gain the benefits of the Martial Adept feat with the following changes (this is labeled under feats as “Fighter Homebrew Bonus” :
      * The Superiority Die is a d8
      * The fighter may learn 4 maneuvers instead of 2
      * Given 2 weeks of downtime, the character may replace one maneuver with another from the list.

Spell Changes:


* Goodberry:
   * Goodberry now consumes its material component


* Create Food and Water:
   * Create Food and Water now requires a piece of non-magical food worth at least five silver pieces, which the spell consumes.
